## Raw Post

This post gives Dan's most precise short-form argument for why AI cannot replace real writers.

The visible official LinkedIn post defines "writer" not as a job title but as a set of deeply human functions:

- sifting through many ideas to find one worth pursuing
- constructing a narrative toward a goal
- capturing and directing attention
- documenting high-signal information for future generations

Dan then makes the AI boundary explicit. AI is not the author. AI is closer to a ghostwriter or production tool. The human still owns the plot, ideas, tone, style, final output, distribution, and sales. That means AI can reduce labor, but it cannot replace executive function.

## Main Position

- writers remain valuable because writing is upstream thinking and narrative control
- AI lacks executive function and still needs a master
- creators should use AI to reduce labor, not surrender authorship

## Business Framing

- distribution and sales are still human-owned layers
- one strong piece of writing can still change everything
- the creator's edge is not volume alone but signal selection and narrative control
