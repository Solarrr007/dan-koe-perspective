## Structured Body

This post compresses Dan's anti-outsourcing stance into a sharper LinkedIn-native framing. The main claim is simple: if the life you want is built from thought, then thinking itself becomes the non-outsourcable layer.

The post is especially useful because the visible official snippet includes Dan's AI advice in unusually explicit form: thinking and writing should not be outsourced to AI. AI should enhance research, writing refinement, and judgment only after the human has already done the first-pass thinking.

This is one of the cleanest support sources for the skill's AI boundary logic. It makes the distinction between enhancement and replacement much harder to misread.

## Core Thesis

- Thinking is the root capability behind building the life you want.
- Thinking and writing should remain human-owned.
- AI is strongest as an enhancer after first-pass thought and judgment exist.

## Key Arguments

- The quality of life outcomes depends on the quality of thinking behind them.
- AI should not write, think, or decide in place of the user.
- The right use of AI is sequential: think first, then enhance.
